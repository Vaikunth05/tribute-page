Dr. M S Swaminathan

Father of Green Revolution in India

Here is a time line of Dr. M S Swaminathan:

  • 1925 - Born in Kumbakonam, India.
  • 1940 - After completeing high school. He was sent to Travancore, Kerala by his mother for education in medicine.
  • 1943 - Witnessing the impacts of Bengal Famine, he devotes to ensure India has enough food. So, he chose agriculture.
  • 1944 - Graduates with BSc in Zoology from University of Kerala, Thituvananthapuram.
  • 1944 - Joins Agricultural College, Coimbatore for another BSc in Agricultural Science. There he won almost every award.
  • 1947 - Moved to IARI to focus on plant genetics and breeding. He specialised in cytogenetics to help crop improvement focusing on genus Solanum of potato, and gains a post-graduate in it.
  • 1949 - UNESCO offers him an 8 month fellowship in Wegeningen University, Netherlands to research ways to combat parasite affecting potato crops. He succeeded in preventing crop infestation and enabled them to withstand cold weather.
  • 1950 - Moves to University of Cambridge's School of Agriculture to study at the Plant Breeding Institute for his Doctoral study
  • 1952 - Earns Phd. for his thesiis "Species Differentiation, and the Nature of Polyploidy in certain species of the genus Solanum - section Tuberarium"
  • 1954 - Joins IARI as assistant cytogeneticist and Collaborates with Norma Borlaug to research on new variety of high yielding wheat crop.
  • 1964 - Continues his research on high-yield, good quality, and disease free wheat crop to help mitigate the mass starvation.
  • 1968 - the wheat production increased from 12 million tonnes to 17 million tonnnes
  • 1970 - pioneered the Green Revolution, tripling the wheat production in 4 crop seasons
  • 1972 - Appointed as the director-general of the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR)
  • 1979 - He was made the secretary of agriculture to the government of India
  • 1982 - he was made the first Asian director general of the International Rice Research Institute (IRRI) in the Philippines
  • 1987 - Awarded the first World Food Prize
  • 1988 - M.S. Swaminathan Research Foundation in Chennai
  • 2007 - President A.P.J. Abdul Kalam nominated Swaminathan to the Rajya Sabha.
  • 1955 - Marries Mina Bhoothalingam whome he met in 1952 while studying at Cambridge. They hav ethree daughters.
  • 2023 - dies at the age of 98

"At 80, M.S. retains all the energy and idealism of his youth, and he continues to inspire good behaviour and more idealism from millions of his fellow human beings on this Earth. For that, we can all be thankful"

--Bruce Alberts, President of the U.S. National Academy of Sciences
